🎊 Family Event Coordination System

Complete Wedding Planning & Management Platform

📋 System Overview

A comprehensive digital platform for coordinating all aspects of a family wedding or major event. Designed specifically for Indian weddings with multiple stakeholders, complex vendor management, and privacy-aware guest tracking.

🎯 Core Purpose: This system manages people, events, vendors, guests, accommodation, and gifts across a multi-day wedding with bride-side and groom-side families working collaboratively while maintaining privacy.

System Architecture

The system is organized into 6 interconnected domains:

👥 People Management

Users, Families, Family Members, and Relationships. Build your family tree and manage who has access.

💍 Wedding & Events

Define the wedding, create sides (bride/groom), assign roles, and plan sub-events (Mehendi, Sangeet, Ceremony, etc.).

👨‍💼 Vendors & Finance

Track vendors by category, create bookings, manage payments, and monitor costs per event.

👫 Guest Management

Maintain guest lists, send invitations per sub-event, track RSVPs, dietary needs, and accessibility.

🏨 Accommodation

Manage properties, rooms, and guest assignments with check-in/check-out dates.

🎵 Entertainment & Gifts

Plan performances and performers, track gift tracking (shagun, cash, jewelry).

Multi-Sided Privacy Model

This system uses a permission-based model where:

  • Bride Side and Groom Side can each manage their own data independently
  • Joint Events can be marked for collaboration between sides
  • Private Events remain visible only to the hosting side
  • Gifts are strictly private to the recording side

✨ Key Features

1. Family & User Management

  • Phone-first user identity (mobile-first design)
  • Family unit organization with multiple members
  • Family relationship graphs (parent, sibling, cousin, etc.)
  • Role-based access control (Owner, Co-organizer, Viewer)

2. Event Orchestration

  • Multi-day wedding management
  • Unlimited sub-events (Mehendi, Sangeet, Ceremony, Reception, etc.)
  • Private vs. Joint event scoping
  • Event scheduling with times, locations, and leads
  • Customizable event types

3. Vendor & Finance Tracking

  • Hierarchical vendor categories (Catering > North Indian, etc.)
  • Contact and quote management
  • Booking status tracking (pending, confirmed, completed)
  • Payment milestone tracking (advance, balance)
  • Cost analysis per sub-event

4. Guest Management

  • Guest database separate from family members
  • Per-guest dietary restrictions and accessibility needs
  • Event-wise invitations (not everyone invited to all events)
  • RSVP status tracking (pending, accepted, declined)
  • Plus-one management

5. Accommodation Management

  • Multiple properties (hotels, family homes, resorts)
  • Room-level inventory
  • Guest-to-room assignments with dates
  • Room type and amenity tracking

6. Entertainment Planning

  • Performances (dances, speeches, skits, etc.)
  • Performer assignments (family members or guests)
  • Costume notes and rehearsal tracking

7. Gift Tracking

  • Shagun tracking (envelopes received)
  • Gift types (cash, jewelry, items)
  • Donor identification (linked to guests)
  • Acknowledgment status tracking
  • Privacy: Strictly visible to recording side only

8. Task & Requirement Management

  • Pre-seeded requirement templates per event type
  • Auto-generation of checklists per sub-event
  • Task assignment to family members
  • Status tracking (pending, in progress, completed)
  • Due date management

🏗️ Database Schema Design

Entity Relationship Overview

Core Entity Relationships:

Users (phone-based)
  ├─ 1:N → Families (owner)
  ├─ N:M → Sides (via SideMembership)
  └─ 0:1 → FamilyMembers

Families
  ├─ 1:N → FamilyMembers
  └─ 1:1 → Sides (one family per side)

FamilyMembers
  ├─ N:M → FamilyMembers (via Relationships)
  ├─ 1:N → SubEventRequirements
  ├─ 1:N → Performances (as lead)
  └─ 1:N → Performers

Wedding
  ├─ 1:N → Sides
  ├─ 1:N → SubEvents
  ├─ 1:N → Properties
  └─ 1:N → Gifts

SubEvents
  ├─ 1:N → Bookings
  ├─ 1:N → GuestInvitations
  ├─ 1:N → SubEventRequirements
  └─ 1:N → Performances

Vendors ──→ Bookings ←─ SubEvents
Guests ──→ GuestInvitations ←─ SubEvents
Rooms ──→ RoomAssignments ←─ Guests

Schema Categories

👥 PEOPLE & FAMILY (4 tables)

Users

Account holders with phone numbers as primary identity

Fields: user_id, phone, email, display_name, timestamps

Families

Family units with an owner user and home city

Fields: family_id, family_name, owner_user_id, home_city

FamilyMembers

Individual family members with optional user accounts

Fields: member_id, family_id, user_id, full_name, relationship_to_owner, is_decision_maker

Relationships

Member-to-member relationship edges within families

Fields: relationship_id, from_member_id, to_member_id, relationship_type

💍 WEDDING & EVENTS (4 tables)

Wedding

Top-level container for the entire wedding

Fields: wedding_id, name, dates, city, status, is_two_sided

Sides

Bride/Groom/Solo sides linking families to wedding

Fields: side_id, wedding_id, family_id, side_name, side_type

SideMembership

User roles on each side (Owner/CoOrganizer/Viewer)

Fields: membership_id, user_id, side_id, role, joined_at

SubEvents

Individual functions (Mehendi, Sangeet, Ceremony, etc.)

Fields: sub_event_id, wedding_id, event_type, dates/times, hosting_scope (private/joint)

👨‍💼 VENDORS & FINANCE (3 tables)

VendorCategories

Pre-seeded hierarchical vendor categories

Fields: category_id, category_name, parent_category_id

Vendors

Vendor contact records by category

Fields: vendor_id, name, category_id, contact, phone, email, address, created_by_side_id

Bookings

Vendor bookings for specific sub-events

Fields: booking_id, vendor_id, sub_event_id, amount, advance, payment_status, booking_status

👫 GUESTS & INVITATIONS (2 tables)

Guests

Guest database separate from family members

Fields: guest_id, name, phone, email, invited_by_side_id, dietary_restrictions, accessibility_needs

GuestInvitations

Event-wise guest invitations with RSVPs

Fields: invitation_id, guest_id, sub_event_id, status (pending/accepted/declined), plus_ones

🏨 ACCOMMODATION (3 tables)

Properties

Hotels, homes, resorts for guest stays

Fields: property_id, wedding_id, name, address, capacity, check-in/out dates

Rooms

Individual rooms within properties

Fields: room_id, property_id, room_number, type, capacity, amenities

RoomAssignments

Guest-to-room assignments with dates

Fields: assignment_id, room_id, guest_id, check_in, check_out

🎵 ENTERTAINMENT (2 tables)

Performances

Performance items (dances, speeches) for events

Fields: performance_id, sub_event_id, name, type, duration

Performers

Family members or guests in performances

Fields: performer_id, performance_id, member_id/guest_id, role, costume_notes

📋 TASKS & REQUIREMENTS (2 tables)

RequirementTemplates

Pre-seeded checklists per event type

Fields: template_id, event_type, requirement_description, priority

SubEventRequirements

Instantiated tasks for each sub-event

Fields: requirement_id, sub_event_id, description, responsible_member_id, status, due_date

🎁 GIFTS (1 table)

Gifts

Shagun tracking - cash, jewelry, items received

Fields: gift_id, wedding_id, recorded_by_side_id, linked_guest_id, gift_type, amount/description, date_received, acknowledgment_status

Privacy: OWN SIDE ONLY - Never visible to other side

🚀 Quick Start Guide

👉 Getting Started in 10 Steps

  1. Delete Example Data
    Remove all light grey example rows from entity sheets to start fresh with your wedding
  2. Add Users (USR-###)
    Create user records for all family members who need access (requires phone number)

    Add bride and groom families. Each family gets one Family record.

    Create member-to-member relationships (parent, sibling, cousin, etc.) within families

    Add one Wedding record with start/end dates, city, and status

    Create one Side per family (typically Bride Side and Groom Side)

    Assign users to sides with roles (Owner, Co-organizer, Viewer)

    Create Mehendi, Sangeet, Ceremony, Reception, etc. with dates, times, locations
  10. Add Supporting Data
    Vendors, Guests, Rooms, Requirements - in the order that makes sense for your workflow

🔒 Privacy & Security

Privacy Model Overview

This system is built with strict privacy controls to respect the sensitive nature of wedding planning:

Core Principle: Each side (Bride/Groom) operates independently but can collaborate on joint events. Sensitive data like gifts is completely isolated per side.

Privacy Tiers

🌍 GLOBAL (Visible to Both Sides)

1

Basic Wedding Info

Wedding dates, primary city, basic event structure

2

Reference Data

Vendor categories, requirement templates, lookup tables

👥 SIDE INTERNAL (Only Own Side)

1

Family Data

Family members, relationships, user memberships on side

2

Side-Owned Vendors

Vendors created by your side (can optionally share)

3

Guest Invitations

Guests invited by your side and their RSVPs

📍 EVENT-SCOPED (Per Sub-Event Privacy)

1

Private Events

Visible only to hosting side (Mehendi to Bride side, Baraat to Groom side)

2

Joint Events

Visible to both sides (Ceremony, Reception, Joint Sangeet)

🚨 STRICT PRIVACY: GIFTS

Gifts table is COMPLETELY PRIVATE to recording side only. Never visible to other side. This includes:

  • No side-to-side sharing of gift records
  • No "who sent what" visibility across sides
  • Each side maintains their own gift tracking independently

Role-Based Access Control

Role Capabilities Side Data Access
Owner Full edit, invite users, manage all data Complete (including Gifts)
Co-organizer Edit most data, add guests/vendors Most data (may exclude Gifts)
Viewer Read-only access for planning input Read-only (excludes Gifts, sensitive data)

Data Ownership Rules

  • Vendors: Owned by creating side, can optionally be shared with other side
  • Guests: Owned by inviting side, no cross-side visibility
  • Bookings: Visible to both sides (needed for event planning)
  • Properties: Owned by managing side, shared for event attendance
  • Gifts: STRICTLY PRIVATE - No sharing, no cross-side visibility

Best Practices

1

Minimize Side Memberships

Only grant access to users who need it on each side

2

Use Appropriate Roles

Don't grant Owner role when Viewer/Co-organizer would suffice

3

Review Joint Events

Clearly mark which events are joint (Ceremony, Reception) vs. private

4

Track Gift Recording

Only record gifts in your side's Gift table - maintain independent tracking
